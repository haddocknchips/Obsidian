### Summary

- This concept note proposes a **Cefiderocol Volume Guarantee** partnership between **GARDP** and **MedAccess** to **expand and accelerate access to cefiderocol** (a novel “Reserve” antibiotic for serious drug-resistant Gram-negative infections) in **low- and middle-income countries (LMICs)** by **reducing manufacturer risk, enabling lower prices, and creating more predictable demand**. The note is positioned to seek support—particularly aligned with **Sweden/Sida priorities**. 

### 1) The challenge with new antibiotics

- **AMR is a major and growing global health crisis** (1.14M deaths in 2021; large projected increases by 2050).
- **MDR Gram-negative infections** (notably carbapenem-resistant strains) cause severe disease (sepsis, pneumonia, cUTIs, bloodstream infections) and disproportionately affect **LMICs**, with heightened impact in **South Asia**, and on **women and children**.
- **Market and regulatory barriers** (fragmented procurement, uneven systems, varied guidelines) make it difficult to introduce affordable new antibiotics in LMICs.
- **Cefiderocol** faces key access barriers: **high manufacturing costs, uncertain demand, lengthy regulatory pathways**.
- GARDP holds rights (licensed by **Shionogi**, 2022) to enable access across **135 countries** (including all LMICs) and has a **sublicense with Orchid Pharma** (2023) to manufacture for these markets.
- Orchid plans **$22–24M** capex investment, but lack of guaranteed demand risks a **high price–low volume trap**; the note argues **de-risking/predictable demand is critical**.
	- _PS: any possibility for a [[Financial counterparts|DFC]] or other sourced CAPEX or W/C financing?_

### 2) Planned response and impact

- Proposed solution: a **volume guarantee** (by MedAccess, supported by GARDP) to create demand/revenue visibility and reduce Orchid’s investment risk → **lower prices and faster access**.
- **Two phases**:
    - **Phase 1 (Year 1, 12 months):** due diligence, market analysis, confirm cost structure and demand scenarios, and develop the guarantee agreement; decision gate to proceed.
    - **Phase 2 (Year 2–6):** negotiate andķimplement the volume guarantee; MedAccess leads execution; GARDP coordinates compliance across partners (licensing, stewardship, sales tracking, royalties, supply oversight).
- Projected outcomes mentioned:
    - Over 4 years: **28,100 patients reached**, **25,000 additional** who otherwise wouldn’t have access.
    - 2028–2031: supply up to **1.8M patients (vials)** across **13 LMICs**, estimated guarantee value **$42.5M** (India-only scenario: **1.1M vials**, **$26.4M**).
    - Expected **56% price reduction**, with **up to $53.7M** savings for procurers.
- Broader intent: create a **replicable “pathfinder” model** for other antibiotics in GARDP’s portfolio and strengthen stewardship/procurement systems.

### 3) Alignment with Sweden’s Strategy and Priorities

- Frames the initiative as supporting Sweden’s goals to **tackle AMR**, **reduce global health inequalities**, and strengthen sustainable health systems.
- Aligns to Sida operational strategy (2024–2026) and Sweden’s development cooperation strategy (2025–2029) via:
    - strengthening foundations for good health (affordable access to last-line antibiotics),
    - building capacity to prevent/manage global health threats (stewardship and lab-guided prescribing),
    - leveraging collaborative partnerships (with/alongside Global Fund, Gavi, Unitaid, UNICEF, regional bodies).

### 4) Timeline & Budget

- Total budget (Phase I + II): **$2.8M over 6 years**.
- Funding ask: **~$530k for Phase 1** over 12 months, starting **Q3 2026**.
- Phase 2 estimate: **$2.3M** (incl. risk charge), contingent on Phase 1 results.
- Notes existing supporters: Germany, Netherlands, Switzerland, European Commission, GSK; seeking additional partners.

### 5) About the partners

- **GARDP:** not-for-profit (created by WHO & DNDi, 2016); license holder managing partners for registration/distribution and implementation support.
- **MedAccess:** social enterprise (2017) using market-shaping tools (incl. volume guarantees) backed by **$200M** capital from BII; will structure and oversee the guarantee.

### Appendix 1 (Activities & Outputs)

- Phase 1 workstreams include: Orchid **COGS deep dive**, E&S/Business Integrity diligence, **demand validation** (focus on India), **transaction structuring** (price/volume matrix), implementation planning.
- Phase 2 includes: transaction execution, impact monitoring (quarterly reviews), implementation support (country plans, uptake reports), impact/learning products; plus GARDP oversight/compliance and data portal for monitoring.

## Key takeaways

- The document’s core argument is that **cefiderocol access in LMICs is constrained less by clinical need and more by market failures** (uncertain demand + high upfront investment), and a **volume guarantee is the proposed de-risking mechanism** to unlock affordability and availability.
- The proposal is **structured like an investment/market-shaping transaction**, with a clear **Phase 1 “go/no-go” diligence gate**before committing to multi-year implementation.
- Intended results are **large-scale access and significant price reduction**, while also strengthening **stewardship and procurement systems** to protect antibiotic effectiveness.
- The note is crafted to fit **Sida/Sweden strategic priorities**, positioning Sweden as catalytic funder for Phase 1 and potentially beyond.
