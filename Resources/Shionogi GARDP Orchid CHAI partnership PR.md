# [[https://gardp.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/GARDP_Cefiderocol_sublicense_PR20230912.pdf|PR]] to accelerate SBI access

## Released 12 Sep 2023

### Notes
 
- Cefiderocol sublicense agreement
- Access objective to predominantly low- and middle-income countries pending local authorisation or national regulatory approval
- Project launched in June 2022
- Target countries have introduction delays of more than a decade usually [similar to vaccines]. Addressing barriers to availability
- Objective to reduce access gap between high- and lower-income countries
- Shinogi-GARDP [[https://gardp.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/License-and-Technology-Transfer-Agreement-1.pdf|licensing and tech transfer agreement]]: 135 countries (~70% of world) including those with the highest AMR burden
- Approved by FDA (2019) and European Medicines Agency (2020) and part of WHO EML
- Orchid will submit the product to the WHO PQ programme; products in PQ eligible for accelerated registration
	- Reducing time for national regulatory approvals to 90 days in participating countries.
	- March 2023: WHO PQ invited manufacturers to submit expresions of interst [how this this sit with Orchid?]
- Manufacturing [[https://gardp.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/GARDP-Orchid-Manufacturing-Sublicense-Agreement_Final_Redacted-Version.pdf|sublicense]] (with Orchid) builds on license agreement between Shionogi and GARDP and on collaboration agreement between Shionogi, GARDP, CHAI
- CHAI will facilitate tech transfer between Shionogi and Orchid (a manufacturer of ceplalosporin antibiotics)
