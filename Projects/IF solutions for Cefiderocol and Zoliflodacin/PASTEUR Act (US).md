### What it is

The **Pioneering Antimicrobial Subscriptions to End Upsurging Resistance (PASTEUR) Act** proposes a **fully delinked federal subscription model**. It has been reintroduced multiple times (most recently 2026) but remains **unenacted**. [[cidrap.umn.edu]](https://www.cidrap.umn.edu/antimicrobial-stewardship/updated-pasteur-act-reintroduced-congress-boost-antibiotic-development), [[congress.gov]](https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/senate-bill/1355)

### Core features

- HHS enters **subscription contracts** for “critical‑need antimicrobials”
- Annual payments of **USD 75–300 million per product**
- Contracts up to **10 years**
- Payments based on:
    - Clinical innovation
    - Public health value
    - System benefit (not sales)

### Pull‑mechanism characteristics

- **Strong, delinked pull**
- Near‑equivalent to a **market entry reward paid over time**
- Explicit stewardship and access provisions

### Limitations / debates

- High fiscal visibility (CBO scrutiny)
- U.S.-centric; global coordination unresolved
- Eligibility and prioritisation governance is complex [[idsociety.org]](https://www.idsociety.org/news--publications-new/articles/2026/newly-introduced-legislation-provides-pathway-to-spur-antimicrobial-development/), [[pharmacytimes.com]](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/is-the-pasteur-act-the-correct-way-to-tackle-antibiotic-resistance-)

**Bottom line:** PASTEUR is the **clearest embodiment of the “gold standard” pull mechanism**, but remains politically stalled.
