### What it is

The UK became the **first country to implement a national delinked subscription model**, following pilots launched in **2022** and scaled nationally from **2024**. [[nice.org.uk]](https://www.nice.org.uk/what-nice-does/life-sciences-how-to-get-your-product-to-market/a-new-model-for-evaluating-and-purchasing-antimicrobials-in-the-uk), [[pharmaceut...ournal.com]](https://pharmaceutical-journal.com/article/feature/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-nhs-antimicrobial-resistance-subscription-model)

### Core features

- NHS pays a **fixed annual fee** for access to qualifying antibiotics
- Value assessed via **NICE health technology assessment (HTA)**
- Initial pilots included:
    - Ceftazidime‑avibactam
    - Cefiderocol
- Contracts typically **~£10m/year per product**

### Pull‑mechanism characteristics

- **Delinked revenue**
- Predictable, modest return
- Explicitly rewards **insurance value** and **option value**

### Limitations

- Scale is **insufficient alone** to drive global R&D
- Primarily secures **availability**, not full R&D economics
- Relies on other countries to co‑invest [[abpi.org.uk]](https://www.abpi.org.uk/media/news/2023/november/uk-antibiotic-subscription-model-key-to-supporting-antibiotic-investment/)

**Bottom line:** A **credible, operational pull mechanism**, but best viewed as a **component of a global basket**, not a standalone solution.
