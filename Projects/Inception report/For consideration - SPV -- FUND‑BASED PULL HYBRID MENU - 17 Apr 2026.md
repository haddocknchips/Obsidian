_(For screening under GARDP Innovative Finance work)_

## Purpose

Provide **delinked or partially delinked pull incentives** for antibiotics **while solving upfront financing and access constraints** through a dedicated **vehicle (SPV / fund / facility)**.

---

## OPTION 1 — Outcome‑Linked Procurement Financing Facility (SECURE Core Model)

**What it is**  
A **results‑based pre‑financing facility** that provides upfront capital for antibiotic procurement, with **repayment by countries triggered only if predefined outputs are achieved**.

**Vehicle / SPV role**

- Aggregates investor capital (MDBs, DFIs, impact investors)
- Channels concessional loans to countries for antibiotic provision
- Manages repayment triggers and cashflows

**Pull logic**

- **Indirect pull** via guaranteed procurement and predictable demand
- Compatible with **delinked pricing** (revenue not driven by volumes)

**Risk‑sharing**

- **50% first‑loss guarantee** from development funders
- Grant subsidies to cover **investor interest / premium**
- Country repayment only if outputs (e.g. timely delivery, quality, registration) are met

**Best fit in value chain**

- Procurement → Access → Stewardship

**Why it matters**

- Enables countries to “test” access with limited upfront risk
- Mobilises private capital without tying returns to antibiotic sales volume

---

## OPTION 2 — Pooled Subscription / Minimum Revenue Guarantee Fund

**What it is**  
A **pooled, multi‑country subscription fund** that provides **fixed, predictable compensation** to antibiotic suppliers, **partially or fully delinked from sales volumes**.

**Vehicle / SPV role**

- Collects country subscriptions (tiered by capacity)
- Aggregates demand across countries
- Pays suppliers a **minimum guaranteed revenue** over a defined period

**Pull logic**

- **Explicit pull incentive**
- Revenue certainty independent of unit sales
- Supports stewardship by removing pressure to oversell

**Design features already identified**

- Partial delinkage favoured by some suppliers
- Pooled, multi‑country structure reduces risk of hitting volume caps
- Typical duration discussed: ~10 years
- Compensation set to cover fixed costs + margin

**Best fit in value chain**

- Manufacturing → Commercialisation → Long‑term availability

**Why it matters**

- Closest LMIC‑compatible analogue to subscription / MER models
- Addresses the “commercialisation valley of death” directly

---

## OPTION 3 — Hybrid: Financing Facility + Subscription Layer (Stacked Pull)

**What it is**  
A **two‑layer hybrid** combining:

1. **Outcome‑linked pre‑financing** (Option 1) for early access, and
2. A **pooled subscription / revenue guarantee** (Option 2) for longer‑term supplier viability.

**Vehicle / SPV role**

- Houses both financing and subscription cashflows
- Manages transition from donor‑/investor‑supported phase to country‑funded phase

**Pull logic**

- Short‑term: demand assurance through financed procurement
- Medium‑/long‑term: delinked revenue through subscription payments

**Risk‑sharing**

- Early phase: donor first‑loss + interest subsidies
- Later phase: increasing country contribution

**Best fit in value chain**

- End‑to‑end: manufacturing → procurement → access → sustainability

**Why it matters**

- Matches SECURE’s stated ambition of **declining donor reliance**
- Creates a credible pathway from pilot to scale

---

## OPTION 4 — Hosted SPV on Existing Procurement Platform

**What it is**  
A **financing SPV** layered onto an **existing procurement platform** (rather than a fully stand‑alone structure).

**Vehicle / SPV role**

- Provides financing, guarantees, and payment logic
- Relies on host platform for procurement execution

**Pull logic**

- Same as Options 1–3 (depends on configuration)
- Pull strength derives from **guaranteed access + pooled demand**

**Advantages**

- Lower operational burden
- Faster deployment
- Leverages existing contracting, logistics, and QA capacity

**Key decision point**

- Hosting option explicitly identified as a structuring task (stand‑alone vs existing platform)

## Cross‑Cutting Design Questions (for screening)

|Dimension|Key question|
|---|---|
|Delinkage|Full vs partial? At product or portfolio level?|
|Geography|LMIC‑only vs mixed income pool|
|Duration|5 vs 10 years|
|Compensation|Cost‑covering vs incentive‑bearing|
|Triggers|Output‑based vs outcome‑based|
|Governance|Who sets prices, volumes, and eligibility?|
|GARDP capacity|Can this be managed within existing governance?|

---

## Bottom‑line framing for decision‑makers

- **Option 1** = access‑first, finance‑heavy, weakest “pure pull”
- **Option 2** = strongest pull, simplest conceptually, harder politically
- **Option 3** = most aligned with SECURE vision and GARDP role
- **Option 4** = an implementation choice that reduces friction across all options
