### General DFC Financing Terms

**Types of Debt Financing Offered**

- Working capital
- Term loans
- Guarantees

- Project finance for greenfield or expansion projects (preference to limit DFC’s share of project costs to 50% of the total, although it may go up to 75% for expansion projects)

**Tenor**

- Typical tenors range from 5 – 10 years, but may go up to 15 years in cases where the credit, developmental, and other policy effects so warrant

**Pricing**

- Comparable to market, with goal to complement, not supplant, private sector lending options [PS: scope to get better pricing with policy and strategy alignment?]
- Determined on a case-by-case basis

**Currency**

- Typically denominated in USD, although some local currency options may be available on a case-by-case basis

**Collateral**

- Collateral and security packages are evaluated and structured on a case-by-case basis, with unsecured loans being possible under certain circumstances

- DFC considers pledged assets such as real estate, negative mortgage liens, as well as gross revenue pledges

- Coverage of collateral value to loan amount is typically 1.5 times the fair market value of the assets

**Other**

- For non-greenfield projects, DFC requires audited financials, preferably three years
- DFC typically does not take on technology risk
- Financial covenants will vary by project and financing type

- In project finance transactions, project completion criteria may include but are not limited to the following covenants: debt service-reserve funds, a restriction on additional debt, protection against transferring significant assets, and operational and financial tests (generation of minimum amount of revenue, net income and cash flow) over a pre-determined test period

### General Underwriting Criteria

**Debt Service Capacity**

- Free cash flow for debt service for a set period, e.g. the prior six months, should be approximately 150% of the projected total debt service obligations for the next such period
- Adjustments to this general criterion may apply in situations where cash flows are expected to be volatile or uncertain, including because of FX fluctuations

**Leverage**

- Debt-to-equity should typically be no greater than 60:40

**Management and Governance**

- Strong Board and Management experience and track record are critical
